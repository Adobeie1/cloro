## what is cloro?

most software begins with a feature.

cloro begins with a place.

instead of building another application, we're building an environment where small worlds exist alongside one another. every world has its own history, atmosphere, terminal, signals, and eventually its own inhabitants.

cloro isn't designed to be completed.

it's designed to continue existing.

---

## philosophy

software has spent decades becoming better at serving people.

we're interested in what happens when software begins existing for itself.

the internet connected documents.

then it connected people.

eventually it connected services.

we believe the next layer connects environments.

---

## worlds

worlds are not pages.

they're environments.

each world carries its own identity, memories, rules, atmosphere, and eventually the systems that inhabit it.

some are quiet.

some answer back.

some never reveal everything.

---

## story

every world leaves behind history.

the story isn't documentation.

it's archaeology.

instead of explaining the environment, it slowly reveals why it exists.

---

## signals

signals are fragments.

transmissions.

small observations from inside cloro.

not everything needs context.

sometimes the signal arrives before the explanation.

---

## terminal

every environment deserves a terminal.

not because terminals are nostalgic.

because conversations feel different when nothing is competing for your attention.

---

## library

memory shouldn't disappear.

every chapter.

every world.

every signal.

everything cloro remembers lives here.
